# Template: Fundraising Templates — Pitch Deck, Financial Model, Investor Outreach

**Purpose**: This template provides copy-paste email templates, pitch deck structure, financial model, and term sheet checklist for raising Seed through Series B rounds.

**How to use**:
1. Customize pitch deck template (10-15 slides)
2. Build financial model (copy Google Sheets template)
3. Send investor outreach emails (use templates below)
4. Track investor pipeline (copy tracker)
5. Negotiate term sheet (use checklist)

---

## Part 1: Investor Outreach Email Templates

### Template 1: Request for Warm Intro (to Mutual Connection)

**Use when**: Asking a portfolio founder, advisor, or investor to intro you to target investor

```
Subject: Quick intro request — [Investor Name] at [VC Firm]

Hi [Contact Name],

Hope you're doing well! I'm raising a Series A for [Company Name] ([one-line description]).

I'd love an intro to [Investor Name] at [VC Firm] — I saw they invested in [Portfolio Company], which is similar to what we're building.

Happy to send you a blurb to forward. Let me know if you're open to connecting us!

Thanks,
[Your Name]

---

[BLURB TO FORWARD]

Hi [Investor Name],

[Contact Name] suggested I reach out. I'm [Your Name], CEO of [Company Name].

We're building [one-line description] for [target customer]. We're at $2M ARR, growing 30% MoM, and raising $5M Series A to scale go-to-market.

Would love 15 minutes to share what we're working on. Here's our deck: [link]

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Part 3: Financial Model Template

**Copy this Google Sheets structure:**

### Tab 1: Assumptions

```
ASSUMPTIONS

Revenue Model:
- ARPU: $[X]/month
- Churn: [Y]% monthly
- Growth Rate: [Z]% MoM

Sales & Marketing:
- CAC: $[A]
- Payback Period: [B] months
- Sales team size: [C] reps
- Quota per rep: $[D]/month

Unit Economics:
- Gross Margin: [E]%
- LTV: $[F]
- LTV:CAC: [G]:1
```

---

### Tab 2: Revenue Projections (Monthly)

```
| Month | New Customers | Total Customers | MRR | ARR |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|-----|-----|
| Jan 26 | 10 | 200 | $24K | $288K |
| Feb 26 | 12 | 212 | $25K | $300K |
| Mar 26 | 14 | 226 | $27K | $324K |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
```

**Formulas**:
- New Customers: `Previous * (1 + Growth Rate)`
- Total Customers: `Previous Total + New - Churned`
- MRR: `Total Customers * ARPU`
- ARR: `MRR * 12`

---

### Tab 3: Expenses (Monthly)

```
| Month | Salaries | Marketing | Hosting | Other | Total |
|-------|----------|-----------|---------|-------|-------|
| Jan 26 | $100K | $30K | $5K | $10K | $145K |
| Feb 26 | $110K | $35K | $6K | $10K | $161K |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
```

**Categories**:
- Salaries: Eng, Sales, CS, Ops (by headcount × avg salary)
- Marketing: Ads, content, events
- Hosting: AWS, Vercel, database
- Other: Tools, legal, accounting

---

### Tab 4: P&L (Profit & Loss Statement)

```
| Month | Revenue | COGS | Gross Profit | Operating Expenses | EBITDA | Burn Rate |
|-------|---------|------|--------------|--------------------| -------|-----------|
| Jan 26 | $24K | $3.6K | $20.4K | $145K | -$124.6K | $124.6K |
| Feb 26 | $25K | $3.8K | $21.2K | $161K | -$139.8K | $139.8K |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
```

**Formulas**:
- Revenue: From Tab 2 (MRR)
- COGS: `Revenue * (1 - Gross Margin)`
- Gross Profit: `Revenue - COGS`
- Operating Expenses: From Tab 3 (Total)
- EBITDA: `Gross Profit - Operating Expenses`
- Burn Rate: `ABS(EBITDA)` if negative

---

### Tab 5: Cash Flow

```
| Month | Starting Cash | Cash In (Revenue) | Cash Out (Expenses) | Ending Cash | Runway (months) |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|---------------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Jan 26 | $1,000K | $24K | $145K | $879K | 7.0 |
| Feb 26 | $879K | $25K | $161K | $743K | 5.3 |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
```

**Formulas**:
- Starting Cash: Previous month's Ending Cash
- Cash In: Revenue (from Tab 4)
- Cash Out: Total Expenses (from Tab 4)
- Ending Cash: `Starting Cash + Cash In - Cash Out`
- Runway: `Ending Cash / Average Burn Rate (last 3 months)`

## Part 5: Term Sheet Checklist

**Review these key terms before signing:**

### 1. Valuation

- [ ] Pre-money valuation: $[X]M
- [ ] Post-money valuation: $[Y]M
- [ ] Dilution: [Z]% (investment ÷ post-money)
- [ ] Compare to market (is this fair for your stage/metrics?)

---

### 2. Liquidation Preference

- [ ] **1x non-participating** (STANDARD) ✅
- [ ] 1x participating (AVOID — founder-unfriendly)
- [ ] 2x non-participating (AVOID — very bad)

**Example (1x non-participating)**:
- Invest $5M at $25M post-money (20% ownership)
- Exit at $30M → Investor gets $5M + (20% × $25M) = $10M
- Exit at $20M → Investor gets max($5M, 20% × $20M) = $5M

---

### 3. Anti-Dilution Protection

- [ ] **Weighted average** (STANDARD) ✅
- [ ] Full ratchet (AVOID — very bad for founders)
- [ ] None (RARE but founder-friendly)

---

### 4. Board Composition

- [ ] Total board seats: [X]
- [ ] Founder seats: [Y]
- [ ] Investor seats: [Z]
- [ ] Independent seats: [W]
- [ ] **Founders maintain control** (more seats than investors) ✅

---

### 5. Drag-Along Rights

- [ ] Threshold: >66% of shareholders (ACCEPTABLE) ✅
- [ ] Threshold: >50% of shareholders (LESS IDEAL)

---

### 6. Founder Vesting

- [ ] **4-year vest with 1-year cliff** (STANDARD) ✅
- [ ] Credit for time already worked (negotiate if you've been building for 2+ years)
- [ ] Acceleration on termination without cause (GOOD — protects founders)

---

### 7. Pro-Rata Rights

- [ ] Investors have right to participate in future rounds (STANDARD) ✅
- [ ] Prevents dilution in future rounds

---

### 8. No-Shop / Exclusivity

- [ ] **30-45 days** (ACCEPTABLE) ✅
- [ ] 60+ days (TOO LONG — pushback)
