## Common Onboarding Mistakes in SEA

### Mistake #1: Assuming Customers Will Ask for Help
**What happens**: In MY/ID/TH, customers avoid appearing unknowledgeable. They struggle silently.

**Western approach**: "Reach out if you have questions!"
**SEA approach**: Proactive check-ins with specific offers

**AI Solution**:
```
Prompt: "Draft a proactive check-in for [COUNTRY] customer who hasn't logged in for 5 days. Frame as helpful resources, not 'you're not using the product'."
```

---

### Mistake #2: Email-Only Communication
**What happens**: Email response rates in SEA B2B: 20-30%. WhatsApp/LINE: 85-90%.

**Western approach**: Formal email updates only
**SEA approach**: WhatsApp for quick updates, email for formal milestones

**AI Solution**:
```
Prompt: "Convert this email update into a WhatsApp message for [COUNTRY]. Keep under 75 words, conversational tone."
```

---

### Mistake #3: Rushing the Relationship
**What happens**: In relationship cultures (MY/ID/TH), trust building takes time. Rushing feels transactional.

**Western approach**: "Complete these 5 tasks in Week 1"
**SEA approach**: "Let's start with 1-2 quick wins, then build from there"

**AI Solution**:
```
Prompt: "Create a 90-day onboarding plan for [COUNTRY] that prioritizes relationship building alongside product adoption. Include cultural considerations."
```

---

### Mistake #4: One Champion, No Stakeholder Diversification
**What happens**: Consensus cultures need buy-in from multiple stakeholders. If your champion leaves, you lose the account.

**Western approach**: Work exclusively with one champion
**SEA approach**: Engage champion + executive sponsor + key end users

**AI Solution**:
```
Prompt: "Generate a stakeholder engagement plan for [COMPANY] in [COUNTRY]. Map who to engage when and how to navigate consensus decision-making."
```

---

### Mistake #5: Ignoring Low Usage Early
**What happens**: Low usage in Week 1-2 predicts churn. But in SEA, customers won't tell you they're struggling.

**Western approach**: Wait for customer to reach out
**SEA approach**: Proactive intervention at first sign of low usage

**AI Solution**:
```
Prompt: "Customer [COMPANY] in [COUNTRY] hasn't logged in for 7 days. Draft a face-saving, helpful outreach that offers support without implying they're failing."
```
