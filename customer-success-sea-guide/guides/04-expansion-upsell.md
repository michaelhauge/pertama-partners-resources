## Objection Handling

### Objection #1: "We don't have budget"

**Root cause**: Genuine budget constraint OR unclear ROI

**AI Response Strategy**:
```
Customer says: "We don't have budget for this right now"

Generate objection response:

**Acknowledge**:
- "I understand budget is tight"
- Don't dismiss or pressure

**Diagnose**:
- Ask: "Is it a matter of budget availability, or is the ROI not clear?"
- If budget: "When does budget free up? We can revisit then"
- If ROI: "Let me show you the value calculation"

**ROI Justification** (if unclear value):
- "Based on your current usage, [EXPANSION] would [QUANTIFIED BENEFIT]"
- "The investment of [$X/month] would [SAVE $Y, GENERATE $Z]"
- "Payback period: [X MONTHS]"

**Flexibility**:
- Downsize package: "We could start smaller â€” [REDUCED OPTION]"
- Payment terms: "Quarterly vs annual?"
- Delayed start: "Activate in [NEXT QUARTER] when budget available?"

**Next steps**:
- "What would need to be true for this to fit your budget?"
- "Who else should be involved in the decision?"

Tone: [COUNTRY]-appropriate, patient (don't push)
```

---

### Objection #2: "We're happy with our current plan"

**Root cause**: Don't see need OR unaware of limitations they're hitting

**AI Response Strategy**:
```
Customer says: "We're happy with what we have"

Generate objection response:

**Acknowledge**:
- "That's great to hear!"
- "Glad [CURRENT PLAN] is working well"

**Gentle challenge**:
- "I noticed [USAGE PATTERN or FEATURE REQUEST]. Does that suggest [LIMITATION]?"
- "Many teams don't realize [CAPABILITY] is available until we mention it"

**Educate on opportunity cost**:
- "What you're doing manually in [X], [EXPANSION] would automate"
- "The time your team spends on [WORKAROUND], you could reinvest in [VALUE]"

**Low-pressure offer**:
- "Would it make sense to see a quick demo?"
- "No commitment â€” just want to show you what's possible"

**Alternative**:
- "Totally understand if now isn't the right time"
- "Can I check in again in [30/60 DAYS]?"

Tone: Consultative, not pushy
```

---

### Objection #3: "Let me think about it"

**Root cause**: Need more information OR avoiding decision

**AI Response Strategy**:
```
Customer says: "Let me think about it"

Generate objection response:

**Accept, don't pressure**:
- "Absolutely, take the time you need"

**Diagnose hesitation**:
- "What information would help you decide?"
- "Are there specific concerns I can address?"
- "Who else needs to be involved in the decision?"

**Offer resources**:
- "Would a demo help?"
- "Can I send a case study of [SIMILAR CUSTOMER]?"
- "Happy to put together ROI calculation"

**Set timeline**:
- "When would be a good time to follow up?"
- "Should I check in next week, or give you more time?"

**Respect decision**:
- "If you decide it's not the right fit, that's okay too"
- "I'm here if you have questions"

Tone: Patient, respectful (especially in SEA)
```

## SEA-Specific Expansion Tactics

### Malaysia/Indonesia/Thailand: Relationship-Led Expansion

**Cultural Context**:
- Expansion seen as deepening partnership (not transactional upsell)
- Consensus decision-making (multiple stakeholders)
- Avoid pushy sales tactics (damages relationship)

**Expansion Approach**:
```
Draft relationship-based expansion pitch for [MY/ID/TH].

Context:
- Expansion opportunity: [DESCRIBE]
- Relationship strength: [STRONG / MODERATE]

Generate proposal:

**Framing**:
- "As our partnership grows..."
- "I wanted to share an idea that might help your team"
- Position as partnership evolution, not upsell

**Collaborative discovery**:
- "What are your goals for [NEXT QUARTER/YEAR]?"
- "How can we better support [TEAM]?"
- Let them articulate need (don't push)

**Patience**:
- Allow time for decision (consensus takes time)
- Don't rush: "Take time to discuss with your team"

**Stakeholder engagement**:
- Engage multiple stakeholders (not just champion)
- Executive sponsor buy-in critical

**Cultural language**:
- MY: "I wanted to propose this as we continue working together"
- ID: "Terima kasih for the partnership. I have an idea..."
- TH: "I wanted to respectfully share something that might be helpful"

Tone: Warm, patient, relationship-first
```

**Key**: Expansion strengthens relationship (not just revenue transaction)

**Key**: Can be more direct about expansion (not seen as pushy)
