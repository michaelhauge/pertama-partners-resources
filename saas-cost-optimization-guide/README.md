# CFO's Guide to SaaS Cost Optimization in Southeast Asia

## The $500K+ Problem Hiding in Your SaaS Stack

**The Reality**: Most SEA companies with $2M-$50M ARR are overspending on SaaS by 30-40% ($200K-$2M+ annually).

**The Causes**:
- **Shadow IT**: Departments buying tools without finance approval (20-30% of actual spend)
- **Zombie subscriptions**: Unused tools still being paid for (15-25% of spend)
- **Overlapping tools**: 3 different teams buying 3 different tools that do the same thing
- **Poor negotiation**: Paying list price when 20-30% discounts are standard
- **Currency inefficiency**: Paying in USD when local currency pricing is 10-20% cheaper

**The Solution**: This guide shows you how to recover $200K-$2M+ annually through systematic SaaS cost optimization.

## Quick Start: The 30-Day SaaS Audit

**Don't have 6 weeks?** Start with the 30-day rapid audit:

### Week 1: Discovery
- [ ] Export all credit card/bank transactions (last 12 months)
- [ ] Flag all SaaS vendors (Salesforce, HubSpot, AWS, Google Workspace, etc.)
- [ ] Survey department heads ("What tools does your team use?")
- [ ] Check IT/procurement records (approved vendors)
- [ ] **Deliverable**: Complete vendor list (expect 30-100 vendors)

### Week 2: Usage Analysis
- [ ] Request admin access to top 20 vendors (by spend)
- [ ] Pull usage reports (active users, login frequency, feature usage)
- [ ] Calculate seat utilization (active users √∑ paid seats)
- [ ] Flag unused/underutilized tools (seat utilization <50%)
- [ ] **Deliverable**: Usage analysis spreadsheet

### Week 3: Quick Wins
- [ ] Cancel zombie subscriptions (tools with 0 active users)
- [ ] Downgrade over-provisioned tools (reduce seat count to match usage)
- [ ] Renegotiate expiring contracts (use timing leverage)
- [ ] **Deliverable**: $50K-$200K immediate savings

### Week 4: Strategic Plan
- [ ] Identify consolidation opportunities (overlapping tools)
- [ ] Prioritize by ROI (savings √∑ effort)
- [ ] Create 90-day implementation roadmap
- [ ] **Deliverable**: SaaS optimization roadmap

**Expected outcome**: $50K-$200K savings in 30 days + roadmap for $200K-$1M additional savings over 90 days

## SEA-Specific Considerations

### Singapore
- **Currency**: SGD pricing available from major vendors (Salesforce, HubSpot, Google)
- **Negotiation style**: Professional, data-driven, ROI-focused (similar to US)
- **Tax**: 9% GST on SaaS (ensure vendors charge correctly)
- **Tip**: Singapore companies can often negotiate "APAC HQ pricing" (volume discount across region)

### Malaysia
- **Currency**: MYR pricing rare (most vendors default to USD) ‚Äî opportunity for arbitrage
- **Negotiation style**: Relationship-first (meet in person, build trust before pushing for discount)
- **Tax**: 6% SST on foreign SaaS (but enforcement is inconsistent)
- **Tip**: Use "budget constraints" narrative (works well in relationship cultures)

### Indonesia
- **Currency**: IDR pricing very rare (USD standard) ‚Äî significant FX risk (IDR volatility)
- **Negotiation style**: Relationship-first, patient (don't rush, hierarchy matters)
- **Tax**: 10% VAT on foreign SaaS (must register as digital services provider)
- **Tip**: Multi-year contracts reduce FX exposure (lock in USD/IDR rate)

### Thailand
- **Currency**: THB pricing rare (USD standard)
- **Negotiation style**: Polite, indirect (don't be aggressive, respect hierarchy)
- **Tax**: 7% VAT on foreign SaaS
- **Tip**: Use end-of-year timing (Thai companies have fiscal year-end pressure)

### Vietnam
- **Currency**: VND pricing extremely rare (USD only)
- **Negotiation style**: Practical, outcome-focused (less relationship-dependent than MY/ID/TH)
- **Tax**: 10% VAT on foreign SaaS
- **Tip**: Emphasize long-term partnership (Vietnamese value stability over short-term wins)

### Philippines
- **Currency**: PHP pricing rare (USD standard)
- **Negotiation style**: Warm, friendly, flexible (Filipinos are accommodating)
- **Tax**: 12% VAT on foreign SaaS
- **Tip**: Leverage competitive pressure ("Competitor X offered Y discount")
