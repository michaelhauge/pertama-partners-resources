## Pro Tips for Email Negotiations

### Tip 1: Always Provide Multiple Date/Time Options

**Bad**: "Can we schedule a call?"
**Good**: "Available: [OPTION 1], [OPTION 2], [OPTION 3]"

**Why**: Reduces back-and-forth, shows you're serious.

---

### Tip 2: Use Specific Numbers (Not Ranges)

**Bad**: "We're looking for 20-30% discount"
**Good**: "We need to hit $75K/year (vs your quote of $100K)"

**Why**: Specificity creates urgency and makes negotiation concrete.

---

### Tip 3: Always Mention Alternatives

**Bad**: "Can you give us a discount?"
**Good**: "We've received quotes from [COMPETITOR 1] and [COMPETITOR 2] at 20% lower pricing"

**Why**: Creates competitive pressure (vendors fear losing deals to competitors).

---

### Tip 4: Bundle Leverage Points

**Example**: Stack multiple leverage points in one email:
- Expiring contract (Template 2)
- Multi-year commitment (Template 6)
- End-of-quarter timing (Template 5)
- Reference customer (Template 10)

**Result**: 30-50% total discount (vs 10-20% with single leverage point).

---

### Tip 5: Follow Up Within 24 Hours

**After sending negotiation email**: If vendor doesn't respond in 24 hours, follow up with:
```
Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL SUBJECT]

Hi [VENDOR],

Following up on my email from yesterday. We're on a tight timeline ([DATE] deadline).

Can you provide pricing by [DATE]?

Thanks,
[YOUR NAME]
```

**Why**: Shows urgency, keeps negotiation moving.
