# 4-Week AI Training Program

**Complete curriculum for training your team on AI tools**

This program takes employees from zero AI knowledge to confident, productive AI users in 30 days.

---

## Program Overview

**Duration**: 4 weeks (10-15 hours total time investment per person)
**Format**: Self-paced online + live sessions
**Target Audience**: All employees who will use AI tools
**Prerequisites**: None - starts from basics

**Expected Outcomes**:
- Week 1: Understand AI capabilities and limitations
- Week 2: Use AI for 3-5 daily tasks
- Week 3: Create custom prompts for role-specific work
- Week 4: Confident, independent AI user

---

## Pre-Program Setup (IT/Admin - 2 hours)

**Week Before Training Starts**:

1. **Provision AI tool accounts** for all participants
   - ChatGPT Plus or Claude Pro (recommend both for power users)
   - SSO setup if enterprise tier
   - Test logins work

2. **Prepare training materials**:
   - [ ] Copy prompt library to shared drive ([LOCATION])
   - [ ] Customize data classification guide with company examples
   - [ ] Create Slack channel: #ai-training or #ai-tips
   - [ ] Set up weekly training sessions (Zoom/Teams)

3. **Assign AI Champions** (optional but recommended):
   - Select 2-3 early adopters per department
   - Give them early access (week before official start)
   - They'll support peers during training

4. **Announce program**:
   - Email all participants with schedule
   - Explain "why AI?" and benefits
   - Set expectations: 2-3 hours/week time investment

## Week 1: Foundations & First Prompts

**Time Investment**: 3-4 hours (2 hour live session + 1-2 hours practice)

### Day 1: Launch Event (2 hours - LIVE SESSION)

**0:00-0:15 - Welcome & Why AI?**
- Executive sponsor kick-off (5 min)
- The AI opportunity: Time savings, better quality, new capabilities
- Show before/after examples from pilot users

**0:15-0:45 - AI 101: What It Is and Isn't**
- What large language models can do:
  - Write, summarize, analyze text
  - Extract data, generate ideas
  - Answer questions based on knowledge
- What AI can't do:
  - Access real-time data (unless specified)
  - Remember between sessions (unless you provide context)
  - Replace human judgment and expertise
- Limitations and when to use AI:
  - Best for: Drafts, ideas, research, repetitive tasks
  - Not for: Final decisions, unverified facts, confidential data without proper tools

**1:15-1:45 - Your First Prompts (Hands-On)**
- Everyone logs into their AI tool account
- Instructor demonstrates 5 basic prompts:
  1. "Summarize this article: [paste URL or text]"

  3. "Brainstorm 10 ideas for [topic]"

## Week 3: Advanced Techniques & Customization

**Time Investment**: 3-4 hours (1 hour live session + 2-3 hours practice)

### Day 1: Advanced Skills Workshop (1 hour - LIVE)

**0:00-0:15 - Prompt Engineering Deep Dive**
- Anatomy of a great prompt:
  - Context: Who are you, what's the situation
  - Task: What you want AI to do
  - Format: How to structure the output
  - Constraints: Length, tone, requirements
- Examples: Simple vs advanced prompts
- Live demonstration: Iterating on a prompt

**0:15-0:30 - Multi-Step Workflows**
- Breaking complex tasks into steps
- Example: Research â†’ Outline â†’ Draft â†’ Edit
- Using AI outputs as inputs to next prompt
- Demo: Create a complete blog post workflow

**0:30-0:45 - Handling Limitations**
- When AI gives wrong answers (verify facts)
- When AI is too generic (add specificity)
- When AI ignores instructions (restructure prompt)
- Dealing with hallucinations (spot and fix)

**0:45-1:00 - Q&A and Advanced Tips**
- Custom instructions (ChatGPT) / Projects (Claude)
- Saving and organizing prompts
- AI for AI (use AI to improve your prompts)
- Share power user tips

## Customization Guide

**Adapt this program for your organization**:

### For Small Companies (<25 employees)
- Combine all departments into one session (Week 2)
- Shorter sessions (45 min instead of 1-2 hours)
- More informal (Slack-based instead of formal training)

### For Large Companies (>100 employees)
- Run multiple cohorts (stagger start dates)
- Assign dedicated trainers (not just AI Champions)
- Create role-specific tracks (Sales, Marketing, Ops, etc.)
- Add assessment and certification requirements

### For Technical Teams
- Add deep dive on Code Interpreter, API usage
- Cover prompt engineering for code generation
- Include AI for debugging, documentation, testing

### For Non-Technical Teams
- Emphasize everyday use cases
- More time on basics and troubleshooting
- Focus on tools with visual interfaces

### For Remote Teams
- All sessions virtual
- More async content (videos, written guides)
- Virtual office hours instead of in-person
- Slack/Teams for community building
