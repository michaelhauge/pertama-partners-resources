# Customer Success Program (30 Days)

**Purpose**: Launch customer success program to improve retention and expansion
**Time Investment**: 20-30 hours over 30 days
**Team Size**: 1 CS lead + 1-2 team members
**Output**: CS playbook, customer health scores, expansion pipeline

---

## Why Customer Success Matters

**The Retention Problem**:
- Acquiring new customers costs 5-25x more than retaining existing ones
- 5% increase in retention = 25-95% increase in profits
- For SaaS: Churn is the #1 killer of growth

**Customer Success (CS) solves**:
- **Churn**: Keep customers longer (retention)
- **Expansion**: Upsell and cross-sell (NRR >100%)
- **Advocacy**: Turn customers into promoters (referrals, case studies)

**ROI of CS**:
- **Good CS**: Churn <5%, NRR 110-120%
- **Bad CS**: Churn >10%, NRR 80-90%

---

## Customer Success vs Support

**Customer Support** (Reactive):
- Responds to tickets
- Fixes problems
- Measured by: Response time, CSAT

**Customer Success** (Proactive):
- Prevents problems before they happen
- Drives adoption, retention, expansion
- Measured by: Churn, NRR, product usage

**Example**:
- **Support**: Customer emails "How do I export data?" â†’ Answer ticket
- **Success**: Notice customer hasn't exported data in 30 days â†’ Proactively reach out, show how, identify why they stopped

## Week 1: Foundation & Planning (Days 1-7)

**Theme**: Define CS strategy and customer segments

### Day 1-2: Define Customer Success Goals

**Goals to set**:
- [ ] **Churn target**: <5% monthly churn (SaaS standard)
- [ ] **NRR target**: 100-120% (net revenue retention)
- [ ] **Expansion target**: 20-30% of customers expand (upsell/cross-sell)
- [ ] **Health score target**: 80%+ of customers "green" (healthy)
- [ ] **Product adoption**: 60%+ weekly active users (WAU)

**Example (B2B SaaS)**:
- Starting churn: 8% (too high)
- Target churn: <5% (reduce by 3%)
- Starting NRR: 95% (losing revenue)
- Target NRR: 110% (expansion offsets churn)

**Deliverable**: CS goals document with targets and timelines

---

### Day 3-4: Segment Your Customers

**Why segment?**
Not all customers are equal. Tailor CS approach by segment.

**Segmentation criteria**:
1. **Revenue** (high-value vs low-value)
2. **Product usage** (power users vs inactive)
3. **Risk** (at-risk vs healthy)
4. **Expansion potential** (upsell opportunity)

**Example Segmentation**:

| Segment | Definition | Count | % of Revenue | CS Approach |
|---------|------------|-------|--------------|-------------|
| **Enterprise** | >$50K ARR | 20 | 60% | 1:1 CSM, quarterly reviews |
| **Mid-Market** | $10K-50K ARR | 80 | 30% | 1:many CSM, webinars |
| **SMB** | <$10K ARR | 200 | 10% | Self-service, automated emails |
| **At-Risk** | Low usage, overdue | 30 | 5% | Urgent intervention |

**Deliverable**: Customer segments with CS strategy per segment

---

### Day 5-6: Build Customer Health Score

**Health Score = Measure of customer health (0-100)**

**Components** (weighted):
1. **Product Usage** (40%): Are they using the product regularly?
2. **Engagement** (30%): Are they opening emails, attending webinars, responding to outreach?
3. **Support Tickets** (20%): Are they filing many support tickets (frustrated)?
4. **Commercial** (10%): Are they paying on time? Expanding?

**Example Calculation**:

| Metric | Weight | Score (0-100) | Weighted Score |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Product Usage** (daily active users) | 40% | 80 (8/10 users active) | 32 |
| **Engagement** (email open rate) | 30% | 60 (3/5 emails opened) | 18 |
| **Support Tickets** (low is good) | 20% | 70 (2 tickets last month) | 14 |
| **Commercial** (payment on time) | 10% | 100 (paid on time) | 10 |
| **Health Score** | - | - | **74 (Yellow)** |

**Health Categories**:
- **Green** (80-100): Healthy, expansion opportunity
- **Yellow** (60-79): At-risk, needs attention
- **Red** (<60): Critical, high churn risk

**Deliverable**: Health score spreadsheet for all customers

## Week 2: Customer Onboarding & Adoption (Days 8-14)

**Theme**: Ensure new customers get value fast

### Day 8-9: Map Customer Journey

**Stages of customer journey**:
1. **Onboarding** (Day 1-30): Get set up, first value
2. **Adoption** (Month 1-3): Regular usage, expand use cases
3. **Retention** (Month 3+): Ongoing value, prevent churn
4. **Expansion** (Ongoing): Upsell, cross-sell, referrals

**For each stage, define**:
- [ ] **Goal**: What success looks like
- [ ] **Touchpoints**: CS activities (emails, calls, check-ins)
- [ ] **Metrics**: How to measure success

**Example (Onboarding)**:

| Milestone | Timeline | CS Touchpoint | Success Metric |
|-----------|----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Kickoff call** | Day 1 | 30-min call (set expectations) | 100% complete |
| **Setup complete** | Week 1 | Email tutorial + check-in call | 80% set up |
| **First value** | Week 2 | Guide to first use case | 70% complete first task |
| **Team training** | Week 3 | 1-hour training for team | 5+ users active |
| **30-day review** | Day 30 | Review usage, answer questions | 60% WAU (weekly active) |

**Deliverable**: Customer journey map with touchpoints

---

### Day 10-11: Create Onboarding Playbook

**Onboarding Playbook = Step-by-step guide for new customers**

**Week 1** (Get Set Up):
- [ ] Day 1: Kickoff call (30 min)
  - Confirm use case, goals, success criteria
  - Share onboarding timeline (30-day plan)
  - Assign point of contact (CSM)
- [ ] Day 2-3: Product setup
  - Automated email: "Getting Started Guide"
  - Video tutorial: "How to set up your account"
- [ ] Day 5: Check-in email
  - "How's setup going? Need help?"

**Week 2** (First Value):
- [ ] Day 8: First use case
  - Automated email: "Complete your first [task]"
  - Track completion in CRM
- [ ] Day 10: Check-in call (15 min)
  - "Did you complete first task? Any blockers?"

**Week 3** (Team Adoption):
- [ ] Day 15: Team training (1 hour)
  - Group call with all users
  - Walk through common workflows
- [ ] Day 20: Check-in email
  - "5 tips to get more value from [product]"

**Week 4** (30-Day Review):
- [ ] Day 30: Review call (30 min)
  - Review usage metrics (who's using? how often?)
  - Identify expansion opportunities
  - Address any concerns

**Deliverable**: Onboarding playbook (copy-paste emails, call scripts)

## Week 3: Proactive Engagement & Retention (Days 15-21)

**Theme**: Prevent churn before it happens

### Day 15-16: Identify At-Risk Customers

**Red flags** (signals of churn risk):
1. **Low usage**: <30% WAU (weekly active users)
2. **No logins**: Haven't logged in for 14+ days
3. **Support tickets**: 5+ tickets in last 30 days
4. **No engagement**: Ignoring emails, skipping check-ins
5. **Budget concerns**: "Too expensive", asking for discounts
6. **Champion left**: Key contact left the company

**At-Risk Customer List**:

|----------|--------------|-------------|------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Acme Corp** | 45 (Red) | No logins (30 days) | 30 days ago | 0 | Call immediately, find out why |
| **Startup Inc** | 55 (Red) | Low usage (10% WAU) | 2 days ago | 3 | Training call, identify blockers |
| **BigCo LLC** | 62 (Yellow) | Champion left | 1 day ago | 1 | Meet new champion, re-onboard |

**Deliverable**: At-risk customer list with action plans

### Day 17-18: Reach Out to At-Risk Customers

**Call Script** (At-Risk Customer):

**1. Open with empathy** (not sales):
"Hi [Name], I noticed you haven't logged in much lately. Is everything okay? Any issues we can help with?"

**2. Ask open-ended questions**:
- "What's changed since you signed up?"
- "Are you getting value from [product]?"
- "What would make [product] more useful for you?"

**3. Offer solutions**:
- "Let me show you [feature] that solves [pain point]"
- "Let's schedule a 15-min training for your team"
- "I'll connect you with [support/product] to fix [issue]"

**4. Set next steps**:
- "Let's check in again in 2 weeks. Does [date] work?"
- "I'll send a summary of what we discussed"

**Deliverable**: Reach out to 5-10 at-risk customers, log notes in CRM

### Day 19-20: Build Retention Playbook

**Retention Playbook = How to keep customers engaged**

**Monthly Check-Ins** (for Enterprise customers):
- [ ] Schedule 30-min call every 30 days
- [ ] Review: Usage, goals, blockers, expansion
- [ ] Share: New features, best practices, roadmap

**Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs)** (for top 20% customers):
- [ ] Schedule 1-hour call every 90 days
- [ ] Present: ROI report, usage trends, benchmarks
- [ ] Discuss: Goals for next quarter, expansion opportunities

**Automated Engagement**:
- [ ] Weekly tips email: "Tip of the week: How to [do X]"
- [ ] Monthly newsletter: Product updates, customer stories
- [ ] Webinars: "Best practices for [use case]" (monthly)

**Win-Back Campaign** (for inactive customers):
- [ ] Day 14 of no login: Email "We miss you! Here's what's new"
- [ ] Day 30 of no login: Call from CSM
- [ ] Day 60 of no login: Offer discount or incentive to re-engage

**Deliverable**: Retention playbook with scripts and schedules

### Day 24-25: Expansion Playbook

**Upsell Script**:

**1. Tie to value** (not features):
"I noticed you're using [feature] heavily. The Pro plan includes [advanced feature] which would save you [X hours/week]. Want to see a demo?"

**2. Show ROI**:
"The Pro plan costs $500 more, but you'll save [X hours] which is worth $[Y]. Net ROI: $[Y - 500]."

**3. Offer trial**:
"Try Pro free for 30 days. If you don't see value, downgrade anytime."

**4. Close**:
"Should we upgrade you now, or schedule a demo first?"

**Cross-Sell Script**:

**1. Identify adjacent need**:
"You're using [Product A]. Have you thought about [Product B] for [use case]?"

**2. Show how they work together**:
"Customers who use both see [X% improvement]. Here's how they integrate..."

**3. Offer bundle discount**:
"If you add [Product B] now, I can give you [10% discount] on both."

**Deliverable**: Expansion playbook with scripts

### Day 26-27: Build Advocacy Program

**Advocacy = Turn happy customers into promoters**

**Advocacy Ladder**:
1. **Testimonial**: Written quote (easiest ask)
2. **Case Study**: Full story with metrics (moderate ask)
3. **Referral**: Introduce to peer (high trust ask)
4. **Speaking**: Present at webinar/event (high effort ask)

**Who to ask?**:
- High health score (80+)
- Long tenure (6+ months)
- Vocal supporters (high NPS, positive feedback)

**Advocacy Request Email**:
```
Subject: Would you share your [Product] success story?

- A quick testimonial (2-3 sentences)

- A referral (introduce us to a peer)

Interested? Let's chat: [Calendar Link]

[Your Name]
```

## CS Metrics to Track

**Retention Metrics**:
- **Gross Churn**: % of customers who cancel (target: <5%)
- **Net Churn**: % of revenue lost (can be negative if expansion > churn)
- **NRR (Net Revenue Retention)**: Revenue from existing customers (target: 100-120%)

**Engagement Metrics**:
- **Product Usage**: % of users active (daily, weekly, monthly)
- **Feature Adoption**: % using key features
- **Time to First Value**: Days from signup to first win

**Health Metrics**:
- **Customer Health Score**: 0-100 score (product usage + engagement + commercial)
- **% Green Customers**: Target 70-80%
- **% Red Customers**: Target <10%

**Expansion Metrics**:
- **Expansion MRR**: New revenue from upsells/cross-sells
- **Expansion Rate**: % of customers who expand
- **Time to Expansion**: Months from signup to first expansion

**Advocacy Metrics**:
- **NPS (Net Promoter Score)**: How likely to recommend? (target: 50+)
- **Testimonials**: # of testimonials collected
- **Referrals**: # of referrals from customers
- **Case Studies**: # of case studies published
