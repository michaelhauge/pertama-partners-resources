# Sales Team Onboarding (30 Days)

**Purpose**: Ramp new sales reps to productivity in 30 days
**Time Investment**: 20-30 hours (rep time), 5-10 hours (manager time)
**Team Size**: Sales rep + sales manager + 1-2 mentors
**Output**: Fully ramped rep making first sales by end of month 1

---

## Onboarding Philosophy

**Goal**: Get reps closing deals within 30-45 days (not 90+)

**Key Principles**:
1. **Learn by doing**: Shadowing ‚Üí Practice ‚Üí Real calls (Week 1-4)
2. **Product first**: Reps must deeply understand product before selling
3. **Customer empathy**: Listen to customer calls, read support tickets
4. **Gradual ramp**: Demo calls (Week 2) ‚Üí Discovery (Week 3) ‚Üí Full cycle (Week 4)

---

## Week 1: Foundations (Days 1-7)

**Theme**: Learn the business, product, and customers

### Day 1: Welcome & Setup

**Morning (9am-12pm)**:
- [ ] **Welcome meeting** (30 min) - Manager introduces team, explains role
- [ ] **HR paperwork** (30 min) - Sign contracts, benefits enrollment
- [ ] **IT setup** (1 hour):
  - [ ] Laptop, email, Slack
  - [ ] CRM access (Salesforce, HubSpot, etc.)
  - [ ] Sales tools (Gong, Outreach, LinkedIn Sales Navigator)
  - [ ] Calendar, video conferencing (Zoom, Google Meet)

**Afternoon (1pm-5pm)**:
- [ ] **Read foundational docs** (3 hours):
  - [ ] Company mission, vision, values
  - [ ] Product overview (features, benefits, roadmap)
  - [ ] Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
  - [ ] Buyer personas (who you sell to)
  - [ ] Pricing & packaging
  - [ ] Competitive landscape
- [ ] **Meet the team** (1 hour):
  - [ ] 1:1 with manager (30 min)
  - [ ] Team intro call (30 min)

**Deliverable**: Rep has access to all tools and understands company/product basics

---

### Day 2-3: Product Deep Dive

**Morning (9am-12pm)**:
- [ ] **Product training** (3 hours):
  - [ ] Demo from Product team (1 hour)
  - [ ] Hands-on: Create test account, explore features (1 hour)
  - [ ] Q&A with Product Manager (1 hour)

**Afternoon (1pm-5pm)**:
- [ ] **Use cases training** (2 hours):
  - [ ] Top 5 use cases (who buys, why, how they use it)
  - [ ] Customer success stories (case studies, testimonials)
- [ ] **Shadow customer success calls** (2 hours):
  - [ ] Listen to 2-3 customer onboarding calls
  - [ ] Understand how customers use product

**Deliverable**: Rep can articulate product value and top use cases

---

### Day 4-5: Customer & Market Knowledge

**Morning (9am-12pm)**:
- [ ] **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)** (2 hours):
  - [ ] Company size, industry, location
  - [ ] Budget, decision-makers, buying process
  - [ ] Pain points, goals, success metrics
- [ ] **Buyer personas** (1 hour):
  - [ ] Who are the buyers? (titles, roles, priorities)
  - [ ] How do they evaluate solutions?
  - [ ] Common objections

**Afternoon (1pm-5pm)**:
- [ ] **Competitive analysis** (3 hours):
  - [ ] Who are competitors? (top 3-5)
  - [ ] How do we compare? (features, pricing, positioning)
  - [ ] Battle cards (how to win vs each competitor)
- [ ] **Pricing & packaging** (1 hour):
  - [ ] Pricing tiers, discounts, contract terms
  - [ ] ROI calculator, pricing objections

**Deliverable**: Rep understands ICP, buyers, competitors, and pricing

---

### Day 6-7: Sales Process & Tools

**Day 6 Morning (9am-12pm)**:
- [ ] **Sales process training** (3 hours):
  - [ ] Lead ‚Üí Opportunity ‚Üí Closed Won (your sales stages)
  - [ ] Lead qualification (BANT, MEDDIC, or your framework)
  - [ ] Discovery call ‚Üí Demo ‚Üí Proposal ‚Üí Close
  - [ ] Average deal size, sales cycle length

**Day 6 Afternoon (1pm-5pm)**:
- [ ] **CRM training** (2 hours):
  - [ ] How to log activities, update opportunities
  - [ ] Reporting, dashboards, forecasting
  - [ ] Best practices (data hygiene, notes)
- [ ] **Sales tools** (2 hours):
  - [ ] Outreach / SalesLoft (email sequences)
  - [ ] Gong / Chorus (call recording, coaching)
  - [ ] LinkedIn Sales Navigator (prospecting)

**Day 7**:
- [ ] **Shadow top rep** (full day):
  - [ ] Listen to 3-5 live calls (discovery, demo, close)
  - [ ] Debrief after each call (what went well, what could improve)
  - [ ] Ask questions

**Deliverable**: Rep understands sales process and how to use CRM/tools

---

## Week 2: Practice & Shadowing (Days 8-14)

**Theme**: Build confidence through practice and observation

### Day 8-10: Call Shadowing & Role-Playing

**Daily Schedule**:
- **9am-12pm**: Shadow calls (3-4 calls per day)
  - [ ] Discovery calls
  - [ ] Demo calls
  - [ ] Proposal calls
  - [ ] Objection handling
- **1pm-3pm**: Debrief and take notes
  - [ ] What worked?
  - [ ] What could be improved?
  - [ ] Key questions asked?
  - [ ] How was objection handled?
- **3pm-5pm**: Role-playing
  - [ ] Practice discovery questions
  - [ ] Practice demo (manager acts as prospect)
  - [ ] Practice objection handling

**Deliverable**: Rep has shadowed 10-15 calls and practiced delivering own demo

---

### Day 11-12: Product Demo Certification

**Day 11**:
- [ ] **Demo practice** (full day):
  - [ ] Morning: Practice demo alone (3 hours)
  - [ ] Afternoon: Mock demo with manager (1 hour)
  - [ ] Feedback and iteration (1 hour)
  - [ ] Practice again (2 hours)

**Day 12**:
- [ ] **Demo certification** (manager evaluates):
  - [ ] Can rep confidently deliver full demo?
  - [ ] Can rep answer common questions?
  - [ ] Can rep position value (not just features)?
  - [ ] **Pass/Fail**: Must pass to move to real calls

**Deliverable**: Rep is certified to deliver product demos

---

### Day 13-14: Territory Planning & Lead Research

**Day 13**:
- [ ] **Territory planning** (full day):
  - [ ] Review assigned territory (geography, accounts, industries)
  - [ ] Build account list (50-100 target accounts)
  - [ ] Prioritize accounts (A, B, C tiers based on fit)
  - [ ] Research top 20 accounts (LinkedIn, company websites, news)

**Day 14**:
- [ ] **Outbound research** (full day):
  - [ ] Find decision-makers at target accounts (LinkedIn Sales Navigator)
  - [ ] Draft personalized emails (5-10 templates)
  - [ ] Build call lists
  - [ ] Set up Outreach sequences

**Deliverable**: Rep has 100 target accounts with personalized outreach ready

---

## Week 3: Real Calls (Days 15-21)

**Theme**: Make real calls with manager support

### Day 15-17: Discovery Calls (with manager on call)

**Daily Goal**: 5-8 discovery calls per day

**Call Structure** (30 min):
1. **Intro** (5 min): Build rapport, set agenda
2. **Discovery** (15 min): Ask open-ended questions (pain points, goals, timeline, budget)
3. **Next steps** (5 min): Schedule demo or qualify out
4. **Debrief** (5 min): Manager feedback after each call

**Questions to Practice**:
- "What prompted you to look for a solution now?"
- "What's the impact of this problem on your business?"
- "Walk me through your current process"
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "What does success look like for you?"

**Deliverable**: Rep completes 15-20 discovery calls, books 3-5 demos

---

### Day 18-19: Demo Calls (with manager on call)

**Daily Goal**: 2-3 demo calls per day

**Demo Structure** (45 min):
1. **Recap discovery** (5 min): Confirm pain points, goals
2. **Tailored demo** (25 min): Show features relevant to their use case
3. **Handle questions** (10 min): Address objections, concerns
4. **Next steps** (5 min): Proposal, trial, or follow-up call

**Best Practices**:
- Start with "why" (why this matters) before "how" (how it works)
- Use customer's language (repeat their words back)
- Focus on outcomes, not features
- Ask for feedback during demo ("Does this solve X problem for you?")

**Deliverable**: Rep completes 5-8 demo calls, moves 2-3 to proposal stage

---

### Day 20-21: Proposal & Negotiation (with manager)

**Day 20**:
- [ ] **Proposal training** (half day):
  - [ ] How to build proposals (pricing, terms, SOW)
  - [ ] How to present proposals (walk through, not just send)
  - [ ] How to handle pricing objections
- [ ] **Practice negotiation** (half day):
  - [ ] Role-play common objections
  - [ ] Practice giving discounts (when and how much)
  - [ ] Practice closing language

**Day 21**:
- [ ] **Send 2-3 proposals** (with manager review)
- [ ] **Follow up on past proposals** (from Week 2-3 pipeline)
- [ ] **Practice closing calls** (with manager coaching)

**Deliverable**: Rep sends first proposals and practices negotiation

---

## Week 4: Independence (Days 22-30)

**Theme**: Operate independently, manager spot-checks

### Day 22-25: Full Sales Cycle (solo)

**Daily Activity**:
- [ ] **Prospecting** (1-2 hours): Outbound emails, LinkedIn, cold calls
- [ ] **Discovery calls** (2-3 per day)
- [ ] **Demo calls** (1-2 per day)
- [ ] **Proposal follow-ups** (1-2 per day)
- [ ] **Admin** (1 hour): Update CRM, log notes, forecast

**Manager Check-ins**:
- [ ] Daily standup (15 min): What's on calendar today?
- [ ] Weekly 1:1 (30 min): Pipeline review, coaching

**Deliverable**: Rep manages full sales cycle independently

---

### Day 26-28: First Closed Deal

**Goal**: Close first deal (or get very close)

**Focus**:
- [ ] Identify 1-2 hot deals in pipeline
- [ ] Move them to close (negotiate, address objections, get signature)
- [ ] Celebrate first win! üéâ

**If no close yet**:
- [ ] Review pipeline with manager (why stalled?)
- [ ] Practice closing language
- [ ] Get manager involved to help close

**Deliverable**: First closed deal (or at least verbal commitment)

---

### Day 29-30: Reflection & Planning

**Day 29**:
- [ ] **30-day retrospective** (1 hour with manager):
  - [ ] What went well?
  - [ ] What was challenging?
  - [ ] What do you need more help with?
  - [ ] Set goals for next 30 days

**Day 30**:
- [ ] **Set 60-day goals** (with manager):
  - [ ] Pipeline targets (e.g., $200K pipeline)
  - [ ] Activity targets (e.g., 50 calls/week)
  - [ ] Close targets (e.g., 2-3 deals)
- [ ] **Build 30-60 day plan** (what to focus on next month)

**Deliverable**: 60-day action plan with clear targets

## Common Onboarding Mistakes

### ‚ùå Mistake 1: Too Much Classroom, Not Enough Practice

**Wrong**: 2-3 weeks of training before first call
**Right**: 1 week training, then shadow + practice + real calls

**Why**: Reps learn by doing, not reading. Get them on calls ASAP.

---

### ‚ùå Mistake 2: No Shadowing or Role-Playing

**Wrong**: "Read this doc, now go make calls"
**Right**: Shadow 10-15 calls, role-play 10+ times before solo calls

**Why**: Reps need to see how it's done and practice in safe environment.

---

### ‚ùå Mistake 3: No Clear Milestones

**Wrong**: "Just learn as much as you can"
**Right**: Specific milestones each week (demo cert, first call, first close)

**Why**: Clear milestones create accountability and urgency.

---

### ‚ùå Mistake 4: Manager Not Involved

**Wrong**: "Figure it out yourself" or "Just shadow the team"
**Right**: Manager on every call in Week 3, daily feedback, weekly 1:1

**Why**: Manager coaching is #1 driver of rep success.

## Ramp Curve by Role

### SDR (Sales Development Rep)
- **Week 1-2**: Training + shadowing
- **Week 3-4**: 50% of activity target (e.g., 25 calls/day vs 50)
- **Month 2**: 75% of activity target
- **Month 3+**: 100% of activity target (fully ramped)

### AE (Account Executive - SMB)
- **Month 1**: Training + shadowing, first demos
- **Month 2**: 50% of quota (first closes)
- **Month 3**: 75% of quota
- **Month 4+**: 100% of quota (fully ramped)

### AE (Account Executive - Enterprise)
- **Month 1-2**: Training, shadowing, territory planning
- **Month 3-4**: First demos, building pipeline
- **Month 5-6**: First closes (50% of quota)
- **Month 7-9**: Ramp to 75-100% of quota
- **Month 10+**: 100% of quota (fully ramped)

**Why longer for enterprise?**
- Longer sales cycles (6-12 months)
- More complex deals
- More stakeholders involved
