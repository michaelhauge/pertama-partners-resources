# Investor Update Template (1-Page)

**Purpose**: Send monthly/quarterly updates to investors
**Time to Complete**: 30-60 minutes per update
**Use Case**: Founders communicating with angel investors, VCs, board members
**Output**: Investor email with metrics, progress, asks

---

## Why Send Investor Updates?

**Benefits**:
- **Stay top of mind**: Investors remember you when you update regularly
- **Get help**: Investors can make intros, give advice, recruit talent
- **Build trust**: Transparency = trust, even when things are hard
- **Practice storytelling**: Forces you to reflect on progress

**Frequency**:
- **Pre-seed/Seed**: Monthly (high touch, need support)
- **Series A+**: Quarterly (less frequent, but still valuable)
- **Board members**: Monthly (required)

**Best Practice**: Send on same day each month (e.g., first Friday)

## What to Include (and NOT Include)

### âœ… DO Include:

- **Metrics** (5-8 key metrics with MoM change)
- **Wins** (3-5 highlights, be specific)
- **Challenges** (1-3, be honest)
- **Asks** (1-3 specific requests)
- **Next milestones** (1-3 months ahead)

### âŒ DON'T Include:

- **Fluff**: "We're crushing it!" (show metrics instead)
- **Too much detail**: 10-page essay (keep it under 2 pages)
- **Confidential info**: Salary data, cap table, board conflicts
- **Complaining**: "Competitor X is unfair" (focus on what you're doing)
- **Excuses**: "We missed target because..." (own it, then explain what you're doing)

## Frequency & Timing

### Pre-Seed/Seed (Monthly)
**When**: First Friday of each month
**Why**: High touch, need investor support for intros, hires, advice

### Series A (Monthly or Quarterly)
**When**: Quarterly + monthly for board members
**Why**: Less frequent, but keep investors engaged

### Series B+ (Quarterly)
**When**: After each quarter closes
**Why**: Less dependent on investors, focus on execution

## Special Situations

### ðŸŽ‰ When Things Are Going Well

**Template**:
- Celebrate wins (but stay humble)
- Show metrics (growth, revenue, customers)
- Share what's working (product, sales, team)
- Look ahead (next milestones, hiring, expansion)

**Tone**: Excited but grounded ("We're proud of this progress, but lots more to do")

---

### ðŸ˜“ When Things Are Hard

**Template**:
- Be honest (don't sugarcoat)
- Show what you're doing about it (action plan)
- Ask for help (specific intros, advice)
- Stay confident (we'll figure this out)

**Tone**: Transparent but optimistic ("Facing challenges, but here's our plan")

**Example**:
"MRR grew only 2% this month (target: 10%). Sales cycle is longer than expected (6 months vs 4 months). We're hiring a sales consultant to tighten our process and expect to see improvement by end of Q1. In the meantime, we're focusing on product improvements to reduce churn."

---

### ðŸš¨ When You're in Crisis

**Template**:
- Be direct (don't bury the lede)
- Explain the situation (what happened, why)
- Show your plan (what you're doing)
- Ask for specific help (intros, advice, bridge round)

**Tone**: Urgent but composed ("We need help, here's what we need")

**Example**:
"We have a cash flow issue. Runway is 4 months (down from 12 months) due to slower-than-expected sales. We're cutting burn by 40% (pausing hires, reducing marketing spend) and raising a $1M bridge round. If you know investors interested in bridge rounds or can make warm intros, please let me know ASAP."

## Metrics by Business Model

### SaaS (B2B)
- MRR / ARR
- New customers
- Churn rate
- NRR (Net Revenue Retention)
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
- LTV (Lifetime Value)
- Burn rate, runway

### E-Commerce
- GMV (Gross Merchandise Value)
- Revenue (net revenue after returns)
- Orders
- AOV (Average Order Value)
- Repeat rate
- Gross margin
- Burn rate, runway

### Marketplace
- GMV (Gross Merchandise Value)
- Take rate (% of GMV you keep)
- Buyers / Sellers
- Transactions
- Repeat rate
- Burn rate, runway

### Consumer App
- MAU (Monthly Active Users)
- DAU (Daily Active Users)
- DAU/MAU ratio (stickiness)
- Retention (Day 1, Day 7, Day 30)
- Revenue (in-app purchases, ads)
- Burn rate, runway

## Investor Update Checklist

**Before Sending**:
- [ ] Metrics are accurate (double-check numbers)
- [ ] Wins are specific (not vague)
- [ ] Challenges are honest (don't hide problems)
- [ ] Asks are clear (1-3 specific requests)
- [ ] Tone is appropriate (excited when winning, honest when struggling)
- [ ] Proofread (no typos, formatting issues)
- [ ] BCC all investors (don't expose email addresses)

**After Sending**:
- [ ] Follow up on offers to help (respond within 24 hours)
- [ ] Track responses (who offered intros, advice, etc.)
- [ ] Say thank you (acknowledge help)
