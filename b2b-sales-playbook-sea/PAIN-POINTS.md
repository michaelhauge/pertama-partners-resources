**Week 1-2**: Start with your **highest** score

**Month 2**: Add remaining high-scoring categories

## Special Situation: Multiple Tied Scores

### If A (Qualification) and B (Discovery) both 15+
**Implement together** - they're naturally connected. AI qualification leads to better discovery.

### If C (Proposals) and D (Messaging) both 15+
**Start with Proposals** - higher impact per deal, easier to measure ROI.

### If D (Messaging) and E (Closing) both 15+
**Start with Messaging** - fixes pipeline leakage before closing.

## Industry-Specific Pain Patterns

### SaaS Companies
**Common highest scores**: A (Qualification), C (Proposals)
**Why**: High volume of leads, long sales cycles, complex pricing

**Recommended path**:
1. AI qualification to filter inbound leads
2. AI proposals to handle high volume
3. Discovery/Closing as needed

---

### Professional Services (Consulting, Agencies)
**Common highest scores**: B (Discovery), C (Proposals)
**Why**: Custom solutions require deep discovery, proposals are fully custom

**Recommended path**:
1. AI discovery to understand client needs better
2. AI proposals to speed up scoping
3. Qualification/Messaging as needed

---

### Manufacturing / Distribution
**Common highest scores**: D (Messaging), E (Closing)
**Why**: WhatsApp-heavy in SEA, relationship-based selling, price negotiations

**Recommended path**:
1. WhatsApp workflows to manage conversations
2. SEA closing techniques for relationship selling
3. Discovery/Proposals as needed

## Pain Point Details: What's Actually Happening?

### A1: "We waste time on unqualified leads"

**Symptoms**:
- Discover on call 3 that prospect has no budget
- Find out in final negotiation that you're talking to wrong person
- Spend weeks on deals that were never winnable

**Root cause**: No structured qualification early in process

**AI solution**: AI researches budget signals (recent funding, hiring, tech stack) before first call

---

### A2: "Can't identify all decision-makers"

**Symptoms**:
- Champion says "yes" but then introduces 5 other stakeholders
- Deal stalls waiting for "VP approval" you didn't know existed
- Consensus cultures mean everyone has veto power

**Root cause**: Western BANT assumes individual decision-maker

**AI solution**: BANT-SEA framework identifies all stakeholders, AI researches org chart

---

### A3: "Qualification is inconsistent across reps"

**Symptoms**:
- Each rep has their own qualification criteria
- Sales manager can't compare deal quality across pipeline
- Some reps chase bad deals, others let good ones slip

**Root cause**: No shared qualification framework

**AI solution**: Shared AI prompts = consistent qualification for whole team

---

### B1: "Discovery calls feel generic and unfocused"

**Symptoms**:
- Asking same questions to every prospect
- Not uncovering real pain points
- Prospect says "thanks, we'll think about it" (polite rejection)

**Root cause**: Not doing prospect-specific research

**AI solution**: AI researches each company, generates custom questions

---

### B2: "45+ minutes prepping for discovery calls"

**Symptoms**:
- Reading company website, LinkedIn, news articles
- Scrambling 10 min before call
- Or skipping prep entirely (even worse)

**Root cause**: Manual research is slow

**AI solution**: AI does 80% of research in 10 min, human reviews

---

### C1: "Proposals take 2-3 days to write"

**Symptoms**:
- Research company, write custom Executive Summary, Solution, Implementation
- Internal review loops
- Miss timing window (buyer goes cold)

**Root cause**: Everything is manual, each proposal from scratch

**AI solution**: AI generates first draft in 1-2 hours, human reviews and personalizes

---

### C2: "Proposal quality is inconsistent"

**Symptoms**:
- Senior rep writes amazing proposals, junior rep writes generic ones
- Different formatting, different level of detail
- Customer experience varies by who they get

**Root cause**: No templates, no shared best practices

**AI solution**: Shared AI prompts = consistent quality across team

---

### D1: "WhatsApp conversations fall through the cracks"

**Symptoms**:
- Rep goes on vacation, no one knows what was discussed
- Prospect asks "What's the status?" and rep doesn't remember
- Deals live in WhatsApp, not CRM

**Root cause**: WhatsApp is not integrated with sales process

**AI solution**: AI summarizes WhatsApp threads, logs to CRM

---

### D2: "Responding to WhatsApp takes too long"

**Symptoms**:
- Prospect expects instant response (WhatsApp culture)
- Rep spends 5-10 min crafting each message
- Or sends quick response that's too informal / unprofessional

**Root cause**: Trying to be thoughtful on a fast platform

**AI solution**: AI drafts response in 30 sec, rep personalizes and sends

---

### E1: "Deals stall in final negotiation"

**Symptoms**:
- Sent proposal, weeks of silence
- Prospect says "need to check with boss" (then ghosts)
- Pricing discussions drag on for months

**Root cause**: Western closing techniques assume fast yes/no

**AI solution**: SEA closing framework for relationship-based decisions

---

### E2: "Don't know how to handle common SEA objections"

**Symptoms**:
- "Budget is frozen" (is it really, or soft no?)
- "Need to discuss with team" (when does that happen?)
- "Price is too high" (SEA buyers always say this)

**Root cause**: SEA objections are often indirect communication

**AI solution**: AI objection prep + cultural decoding guide

## Still Not Sure?

### "All my scores are low (< 10 each)"
You might not need AI for sales yet. Your processes are working. Revisit in 3-6 months or when team grows.

### "All my scores are high (15+ each)"
Start with **A (Qualification)**. Fix the top of funnel first. Bad qualification means everything downstream is also broken.

### "My team won't adopt AI"
Read [FAQ.md](./FAQ.md) section on "How do I get my team to actually use AI?" Start with one early adopter, prove results, then expand.

### "I don't have budget for AI tools"
Start with Claude Free or ChatGPT Free (limited messages). Test workflows manually. Upgrade to paid when you hit message limits.
